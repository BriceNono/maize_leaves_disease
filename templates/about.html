About MaizeScan

CNN-based maize leaf disease detection — Graduation Project, ULK Data Science 2025/2026.

Background

Agriculture plays a crucial role in food security in Rwanda and East Africa. Maize is the most widely produced staple crop, yet yield losses from Northern Leaf Blight, Common Rust, and Gray Leaf Spot can reach 30-80% in severe outbreak years. MaizeScan provides farmers and extension officers with an accessible, AI-powered tool for rapid disease identification.

Model Architecture

  • Base: VGG16 (ImageNet, 13 conv + 3 FC layers)
  • Head: GlobalAvgPool → Dense(512,ReLU) → BN → Dropout(0.5) → Dense(256,ReLU) → BN → Dropout(0.3) → Softmax(4)
  • Phase 1: 30 epochs, frozen VGG16, LR = 1e-4
  • Phase 2: 25 epochs, block5 unfrozen, LR = 1e-5
  • Preprocessing: resize 224×224, divide by 255 (rescale=1/255)
  • Optimizer: Adam | Loss: Categorical Cross-Entropy
